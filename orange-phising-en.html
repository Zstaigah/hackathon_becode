What is Phishing?

Understanding the Threat

Phishing is a cyber attack where criminals impersonate legitimate organizations to steal your personal information, passwords, or financial data. These attacks often come through emails, text messages, or fake websites that look convincingly real.

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Warning

Orange will NEVER ask you to provide sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or personal identification through email, SMS, or unsolicited phone calls. If you receive such a request, it's likely a phishing attempt.

Protection Tips

How to Spot Phishing Attempts

üìß

Check the Sender

Always verify the sender's email address carefully. Phishers often use addresses that look similar to legitimate ones but contain small variations or misspellings.

üîó

Hover Over Links

Before clicking any link, hover your mouse over it to see the actual URL. If it doesn't match the legitimate website or looks suspicious, don't click it.

‚ö°

Beware of Urgency

Phishing emails often create a false sense of urgency, claiming your account will be closed or you'll miss an opportunity. Take time to verify before acting.

üîí

Check for HTTPS

Legitimate websites use HTTPS (look for the padlock icon in your browser). However, phishers can also use HTTPS, so this shouldn't be your only check.

‚úâÔ∏è

Poor Grammar & Spelling

Many phishing attempts contain spelling mistakes, grammatical errors, or awkward phrasing that professional companies wouldn't use.

üìé

Suspicious Attachments

Never open attachments from unknown senders. Even if the sender seems familiar, verify through another channel before opening unexpected files.

Real Examples

Common Phishing Scenarios

Fake Account Verification

üö© Red Flag: "Your Orange account has been suspended. Click here to verify your identity within 24 hours or lose access permanently."

‚úì Reality: Orange will never suspend your account without prior communication through official channels and will never ask you to verify through a link in an email.

Prize or Refund Notification

üö© Red Flag: "Congratulations! You've won a free smartphone. Provide your credit card details to cover shipping costs."

‚úì Reality: Legitimate contests don't require credit card information to claim prizes. Orange contests are announced through official channels.

Fake Invoice or Bill

üö© Red Flag: An email with an attached invoice from "Orange Billing" asking you to download and review charges immediately.

‚úì Reality: Check your official Orange account directly through the website or app. Don't download attachments from emails you weren't expecting.

Technical Support Scam

üö© Red Flag: "Your router has a security issue. Click here to install a mandatory security update or call this number immediately."

‚úì Reality: Orange will communicate security updates through official channels and never ask you to install software from email links.

Take Action

What to Do If You Suspect Phishing

  • Don't click on any links or download attachments from suspicious emails
  • Don't reply to the email or provide any personal information
  • Check your Orange account directly by typing the URL into your browser
  • Forward the suspicious email to Orange's security team
  • Delete the phishing email from your inbox and spam folder
  • If you've clicked a link or provided information, change your passwords immediately
  • Enable two-factor authentication on your Orange account for extra security
  • Monitor your account activity regularly for any unauthorized changes
  • Report the phishing attempt to local authorities if financial fraud occurred
